The Process

Four steps to
owning your asset.

Our process is straightforward. From first contact to asset delivery, here is exactly what happens — and what is expected from you at each stage.

1

Choose Your Asset & Tier

Select from our four asset categories and decide between Tier A (higher deposit, shorter contract) or Tier B (lower deposit, longer contract). Both end in full ownership.

2

Present Your Guarantor

Every plan requires one guarantor who signs a Deed of Indemnity for the full contract value. Full requirements are explained in the system section below.

3

Pay Deposit & Sign Agreement

Pay your initial deposit to secure the plan. Both you and your guarantor sign the hire purchase agreement. This is your Day-1 commitment — and ours.

4

Receive Your Asset & Remit Weekly

Your asset is sourced, vetted, and delivered. Make your weekly remittances until the contract is complete — then full legal ownership transfers to you.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions.

Everything you need to know about our hire purchase plans before you apply.

What is the difference between Tier A and Tier B?
Tier A requires a higher initial deposit and results in a shorter contract duration. Because more equity is paid upfront, the total contract value is slightly lower. Tier B has a lower initial deposit, a longer contract duration, and a slightly higher total contract value — reflecting the extended risk period. Both tiers end in the same outcome: full legal ownership of the asset once the final remittance is made. If you are unsure which tier suits you, we will help you decide on the verification call based on your income and capacity.
Do I need a bank account to apply?
A formal bank account is not required for entry-level assets such as the wheelbarrow. For higher-value assets — motorbike, Keke Napep, and automobile — a bank account may be required depending on the remittance method agreed. We accept mobile bank transfers and cash payments across all plan types. Specific payment methods are confirmed at the point of agreement.
What guarantor do I need and what are they responsible for?
You must present one high-quality guarantor before any plan can be finalised. Eligible guarantors are gainfully employed professionals, established business owners, or recognised community or religious leaders. Your guarantor will be required to sign a Deed of Indemnity that covers the full contract value. In the event of default, your guarantor assumes liability for either the return of the asset or the outstanding balance on the contract. This is a firm requirement for all plan types — there are no exceptions.
What happens if I miss a weekly remittance?
One missed weekly remittance triggers an immediate follow-up from our team to understand the situation and agree a resolution. Two consecutive missed payments without resolution will activate our formal recovery procedure: remote immobilization of the asset via GPS (for motorised assets) and formal escalation to the guarantor. Our preference is always to resolve matters directly with the driver — we understand that income can be unpredictable. However, the enforcement framework exists and will be applied when necessary to protect the integrity of the plan.
Can I pay off my plan early?
Yes — and we actively encourage it. Any payment made above the agreed weekly remittance amount is applied directly to the outstanding contract balance, automatically shortening your contract duration. There is no prepayment penalty. Clients who complete their contracts ahead of schedule may be eligible for a fast-track completion incentive — confirm this with our team at the time of your agreement.
Who is responsible for maintenance during the plan?
Maintenance responsibilities are split clearly in the hire purchase agreement. The driver is responsible for all routine consumables and wear items — oil changes, filters, brake pads, tyres, and minor repairs that arise from normal use. Enigma covers major structural failures, specifically engine or gearbox replacement, that occur through no fault of the driver. All assets are fully serviced and roadworthy before delivery, so clients receive a vehicle in good condition from day one.
Can a business apply for multiple assets?
Yes. Transport businesses and logistics operators who require multiple assets can apply for a multi-asset arrangement. Terms are structured on a case-by-case basis, and volume pricing may be available. Contact our team directly to discuss your requirements — we do not advertise standardised fleet pricing as each arrangement is specific to the business and asset type involved.
How long does it take to receive my asset after I pay the deposit?
Enigma operates a demand-driven model — assets are sourced specifically for each confirmed client, not held in standing stock. This means delivery timelines depend on the asset type. Wheelbarrows, being our currently active asset, have the fastest turnaround. For motorbikes, Keke Napep, and automobiles, sourcing and delivery timelines are confirmed at the point of agreement after your deposit is received. We aim to deploy all assets as quickly as possible — we will give you a clear timeline before you commit.
