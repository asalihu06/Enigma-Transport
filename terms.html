Section 1

Definitions

In these Terms and Conditions, the following words and phrases have the meanings set out below unless the context requires otherwise.

"Enigma" / "we" / "us" / "the Company"
Enigma Transport Services, a hire purchase and asset management enterprise based in Abuja, Federal Capital Territory, Nigeria.
"Client" / "you" / "the Driver"
The individual who has applied for and entered into a hire purchase agreement with Enigma Transport Services.
"Asset"
The item of property — being a wheelbarrow, motorbike, Keke Napep (tricycle), or used automobile — that is the subject of the hire purchase agreement.
"Hire Purchase Agreement"
The legally binding contract signed by the Client, the Guarantor, and Enigma, setting out the full terms of the hire purchase arrangement including all financial figures, obligations, and the conditions for ownership transfer.
"Initial Deposit" / "Day-1 Payment"
The upfront equity contribution paid by the Client upon signing the Hire Purchase Agreement, which secures the asset and confirms the plan. Amount varies by asset type and chosen tier.
"Weekly Remittance"
The fixed amount the Client agrees to pay each week for the duration of the contract, as specified in the Hire Purchase Agreement.
"Contract Value"
The total amount payable under the Hire Purchase Agreement, comprising the Initial Deposit plus all Weekly Remittances over the full contract duration.
"Guarantor"
The individual who signs the Deed of Indemnity on behalf of the Client and assumes liability for the full Contract Value in the event of default.
"Deed of Indemnity"
The legal document signed by the Guarantor which sets out their liability and obligations in the event that the Client defaults on the Hire Purchase Agreement.
"Tier A" / "Tier B"
The two structured plan options available for each asset. Tier A carries a higher Initial Deposit with a shorter contract duration. Tier B carries a lower Initial Deposit with a longer contract duration and a slightly higher total Contract Value.
"Default"
The failure by the Client to make two or more consecutive Weekly Remittances without an agreed resolution with Enigma, or any material breach of the Hire Purchase Agreement.

The complete and final terms of your specific plan — including all financial figures, asset details, repayment schedule, and duration — are set out in your individual Hire Purchase Agreement. In the event of any conflict between these general Terms and Conditions and the specific terms of your Hire Purchase Agreement, the Hire Purchase Agreement shall take precedence.

Section 3

Eligibility

To be eligible to enter into a Hire Purchase Agreement with Enigma Transport Services, the Client must:

  • Be at least 18 years of age at the time of application.
  • Be resident in Abuja, Federal Capital Territory, or as otherwise agreed with Enigma in writing.
  • Have a demonstrated or credible source of income sufficient to meet the agreed Weekly Remittance schedule.
  • Present one eligible Guarantor who meets the requirements set out in Section 7 of these Terms.
  • Provide accurate and complete information in their application. Any misrepresentation may result in immediate termination of the Agreement.

Enigma reserves the right to decline any application without being required to give reasons for its decision. Approval of an application does not guarantee that an asset will be deployed until the Initial Deposit is received and the Hire Purchase Agreement is fully executed.

For higher-tier assets (motorbike, Keke Napep, and used automobile), additional eligibility requirements may apply and will be communicated during the verification process.

Section 4

Initial Deposit

Upon signing the Hire Purchase Agreement, the Client is required to pay the Initial Deposit in full before any asset is sourced or deployed. The Initial Deposit amount is fixed based on the chosen asset type and tier (Tier A or Tier B) and is clearly stated in the Hire Purchase Agreement.

The Initial Deposit is non-refundable once the asset has been sourced and deployed. If the Client withdraws from the Agreement after the asset has been sourced but before delivery, Enigma reserves the right to retain the Initial Deposit to cover sourcing and administrative costs.

  • The Initial Deposit is applied directly to the total Contract Value and reduces the outstanding balance from Day 1.
  • Payment of the Initial Deposit must be made via bank transfer or mobile payment as agreed, with evidence provided to Enigma.
  • Enigma will issue written confirmation of receipt of the Initial Deposit.
  • Asset sourcing and deployment will only commence upon confirmed receipt of the full Initial Deposit.

No asset will be deployed and no contract will be active until the Initial Deposit is received in full and confirmed by Enigma. Verbal or informal agreements do not commit either party.

Section 5

Weekly Remittances

From the date agreed in the Hire Purchase Agreement, the Client is obligated to make Weekly Remittance payments as scheduled. The amount, frequency, and total number of payments are fixed in the Agreement and will not change during the contract period unless expressly agreed in writing by both parties.

Payment Method

Weekly Remittances must be made via bank transfer or mobile payment to the account details provided by Enigma in the Hire Purchase Agreement. Each payment must be accompanied by evidence of transfer (screenshot or receipt) submitted to Enigma through the agreed communication channel.

Payment Schedule

Payments are due on the agreed weekly date. If a payment date falls on a public holiday, payment is expected on the next working day unless otherwise agreed.

Excess Payments

Any payment made above the agreed Weekly Remittance amount is applied directly to the outstanding contract balance. This automatically reduces the remaining contract duration. Clients who regularly pay above the scheduled amount will complete their contract sooner and may be eligible for an early completion incentive at Enigma's discretion.

Tracking

Enigma maintains a real-time digital ledger of all payments received against each active contract. Clients may request a statement of their payment history and outstanding balance at any time by contacting Enigma directly.

Section 6

Ownership Transfer

The Asset remains the legal property of Enigma Transport Services for the entire duration of the Hire Purchase Agreement. The Client does not acquire any ownership rights over the Asset until the full Contract Value has been paid.

Upon receipt of the final Weekly Remittance and confirmation that the total Contract Value has been paid in full, Enigma will formally transfer legal ownership of the Asset to the Client. This transfer will be confirmed in writing.

  • The Client may not sell, transfer, sublease, mortgage, or otherwise encumber the Asset during the contract period without the prior written consent of Enigma.
  • The Client may not use the Asset as collateral for any loan or financial arrangement during the contract period.
  • The Client is permitted to use the Asset for its intended commercial purpose during the contract period, provided that use is in accordance with these Terms.

Ownership transfer is unconditional on completion. Once the full Contract Value is paid, Enigma will issue written confirmation of ownership transfer. No additional fees, charges, or conditions will be imposed at this stage beyond what is set out in the original Hire Purchase Agreement.

Section 7

Guarantor Obligations

Every Hire Purchase Agreement at Enigma requires a single, high-quality Guarantor. The Guarantor is not a co-owner of the Asset and acquires no ownership rights. Their role is to provide a legally enforceable accountability layer that protects both the Client and Enigma throughout the contract period.

Guarantor Eligibility

An eligible Guarantor must be one of the following:

  • A gainfully employed professional in full-time or established part-time employment.
  • An established business owner with verifiable business activity.
  • A recognised community or religious leader of standing.

Enigma reserves the right to reject a proposed Guarantor if they do not, in Enigma's reasonable judgement, meet the eligibility criteria. A replacement Guarantor may be proposed by the Client.

Guarantor's Legal Obligations

The Guarantor is required to sign a Deed of Indemnity that covers the full Contract Value. By signing, the Guarantor confirms that:

  • They fully understand the nature and financial scope of the contract they are supporting.
  • In the event that the Client defaults on the Hire Purchase Agreement, the Guarantor assumes primary liability for either the return of the Asset in good condition or the payment of any outstanding balance on the Contract Value.
  • Enigma may escalate directly to the Guarantor after two consecutive missed Weekly Remittances that have not been resolved through engagement with the Client.
  • The Guarantor's liability extends for the full duration of the contract and until either the Contract Value is paid in full or the Asset is returned in satisfactory condition.

The Guarantor obligation is a firm requirement and is not negotiable. No plan will be activated without a signed Deed of Indemnity from an eligible Guarantor. Clients are responsible for ensuring their proposed Guarantor understands the full scope of their liability before signing.

Section 8

Asset Control & GPS Tracking

All motorised assets — motorbikes, Keke Napep, and used automobiles — are fitted with GPS tracking devices and remote engine immobilization systems before delivery. The Client acknowledges and consents to this as a condition of receiving the Asset.

GPS Tracking

Enigma uses GPS tracking to monitor the location and operational status of the Asset throughout the contract period. This data is used for:

  • Verification of asset deployment and use.
  • Recovery of the Asset in the event of default or theft.
  • Enforcement of contract obligations in the event of Default.

GPS data will not be used for any purpose outside of contract management and asset protection. Enigma will not sell or share GPS data with third parties except where required by law or as necessary for asset recovery.

Remote Immobilization

Enigma reserves the right to remotely immobilize the Asset's engine under the following circumstances only:

  • Two consecutive missed Weekly Remittances have occurred and the Client has not engaged with Enigma to agree a resolution.
  • Enigma has reasonable grounds to believe the Asset is being misused, at risk of loss, or removed from the agreed operational area without consent.
  • A formal recovery procedure has been activated following Default.

Enigma will always attempt direct contact and resolution with the Client before activating remote immobilization. Remote immobilization will be reversed promptly upon resolution of the issue that triggered it.

Client Obligations Regarding the GPS Device

  • The Client must not tamper with, remove, disable, or obstruct the GPS tracking device.
  • Any attempt to interfere with the GPS device constitutes a material breach of the Hire Purchase Agreement and may result in immediate termination and recovery of the Asset.
  • The cost of replacing a GPS device that has been damaged or removed by the Client will be borne by the Client.
Section 9

Maintenance & Repairs

All assets are fully serviced and confirmed roadworthy before delivery. From the point of delivery, maintenance responsibilities are shared between the Client and Enigma as follows.

Client Responsibilities

The Client is responsible for all routine maintenance and consumable-related repairs arising from normal use, including but not limited to:

  • Engine oil and oil filter changes.
  • Air filter, fuel filter, and spark plug replacements.
  • Brake pads, brake fluid, and brake adjustments.
  • Tyre wear, puncture repairs, and tyre replacements.
  • Minor bodywork repairs resulting from normal operational use.
  • Battery replacement (for motorised assets).

The Client is expected to maintain the Asset in good working condition throughout the contract period. Neglect of routine maintenance that leads to serious mechanical failure may be treated as a breach of contract.

Enigma Responsibilities

Enigma is responsible for major structural failures that occur through no fault of the Client, specifically:

  • Engine replacement necessitated by internal mechanical failure (not resulting from neglect or misuse).
  • Gearbox replacement under the same conditions.

Enigma reserves the right to assess any major repair claim before committing to coverage. The Client must notify Enigma promptly of any major mechanical issue and must not authorise major repair work without Enigma's prior written approval.

Approved Workshops

For major repairs covered by Enigma, the Client must use a workshop approved or designated by Enigma. Work carried out at unapproved workshops without Enigma's consent may not be covered.

Section 10

Insurance

All motorised assets — motorbikes, Keke Napep, and used automobiles — under an active Hire Purchase Agreement are covered by basic asset insurance for the duration of the contract. The specific terms and coverage limits of the insurance policy will be disclosed at the point of agreement.

  • Insurance covers the asset for the duration of the active contract period only.
  • Once the contract is complete and ownership has transferred to the Client, the Client becomes solely responsible for arranging and maintaining their own insurance.
  • The wheelbarrow asset is not covered by insurance under the standard plan. This may be subject to change and will be communicated if applicable.
  • The Client must not make any insurance claim without notifying Enigma first.
  • Any fraudulent or exaggerated insurance claim by the Client constitutes a material breach of the Hire Purchase Agreement.
Section 11

Default & Recovery

A Default occurs when the Client misses two or more consecutive Weekly Remittances without engaging with Enigma to agree a resolution, or when the Client commits any other material breach of the Hire Purchase Agreement.

Escalation Process

Enigma follows a structured escalation process before taking formal recovery action:

  1. One missed payment: Enigma contacts the Client directly to understand the situation and agree a catch-up plan.
  2. Two consecutive missed payments without resolution: Enigma formally notifies the Client of Default and may activate remote immobilization of motorised assets. The Guarantor is formally notified of the Default.
  3. Continued non-payment following notification: Enigma activates the formal recovery procedure, which may include physical recovery of the Asset.

Consequences of Default

  • The full outstanding balance of the Contract Value becomes immediately due and payable.
  • Enigma is entitled to recover the Asset from the Client without further notice.
  • The Guarantor assumes liability for either the return of the Asset or the payment of the outstanding balance, as set out in the Deed of Indemnity.
  • Any payments made prior to Default are not refundable. They will be applied against the outstanding Contract Value to determine the net amount due.
  • Enigma reserves the right to pursue legal remedies to recover any outstanding amounts through appropriate channels under Nigerian law.

Enigma's preference is always to resolve payment difficulties directly with the Client. Clients experiencing financial hardship are strongly encouraged to contact Enigma immediately rather than allowing missed payments to accumulate. Proactive communication significantly increases the likelihood of an agreed resolution being reached.

Section 12

Early Completion

Clients are permitted and encouraged to complete their contracts ahead of schedule. The following applies to early completion:

  • There is no prepayment penalty. Any amount paid above the agreed Weekly Remittance is applied directly to the outstanding balance, automatically shortening the contract duration.
  • The Client may make a lump sum payment at any time to settle the remaining outstanding balance. Enigma will issue written confirmation of the outstanding balance upon request.
  • Upon receipt of the final payment that clears the Contract Value in full, Enigma will formally confirm completion and initiate the ownership transfer process.
  • Clients who complete their contracts early may be eligible for a fast-track completion incentive at Enigma's discretion. The availability and terms of any such incentive will be confirmed at the time of the early settlement.
Section 13

Termination

Termination by the Client

The Client may request termination of the Hire Purchase Agreement at any time before the full Contract Value is paid. In the event of Client-initiated termination:

  • The Client must return the Asset to Enigma in good working condition, reasonable wear and tear excepted.
  • All payments made to date are non-refundable.
  • If the Asset is returned in a damaged or deteriorated condition beyond normal wear and tear, Enigma may deduct the cost of repair or remediation from any credit due to the Client.
  • No ownership rights transfer to the Client upon termination. The Asset remains the property of Enigma.

Termination by Enigma

Enigma may terminate the Hire Purchase Agreement immediately and without further notice in the following circumstances:

  • The Client has Defaulted on the Agreement as defined in Section 11.
  • The Client has provided false or materially misleading information in their application or during the contract period.
  • The Client has tampered with or removed the GPS tracking device.
  • The Client has used the Asset for purposes materially outside its intended commercial use.
  • The Client has attempted to sell, transfer, or encumber the Asset without Enigma's written consent.

Upon termination by Enigma, the outstanding Contract Value becomes immediately due, and Enigma is entitled to recover the Asset forthwith.
